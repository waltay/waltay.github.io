Places to Visit in Provo

Bridal Veil Falls

Bridal Veil Falls is a 1.4 mile-long trail which features a beautiful 607 foot-tall waterfall. The waterfall gives a similar appearance as a bridal veil, hence the name of the waterfall and the trail. It's a very simple hike suitable for all ages and goes along the Provo River which results in beautiful scenery. At the parking lot there's an area with picnic tables to enjoy a meal at. There used to be a tram which would take people up to a restaurant on the cliffs of the falls. Sadly, the tram was destroyed in an Avalanche and in 2008 a fire burned down most of the restaurant.

Monte L. Bean Life Science Museum

The Monte L. Bean Life Science Museum is located at Brigham Young University. It has collections of vascular and nonvascular plants, vertebrate and invertebrate animals are mantained and made available for researchers and educators. The museum has exhibits which showcase the Earth and the living beings within it. Some of the exhibits include the characteristics of Earth, marine and fresh-water ecosystems, the principles of evolution, threatened, endangered, and extinct life forms, amongst others. There's no entrance cost for the museum. So be sure to drop by and learn more about the Earth you live in.

The Y Trail

If you've been at or passed by Provo, you'll have likely seen the iconic white Y on the mountain. Well, there's a trail you can hike that will take you all the way to that big white "Y". The story of why there's a Y on the mountain is that a little over a 100 years ago, the junior class of Brigham Young High wanted to paint on the mountain the year in which they would graduate. The senior class got upset over this and to settle the conflict, the President of Brigham Young University and the Principal of the high school opted for painting BYU on the mountain instead. On the day they would take on the task of doing this, a line of high school and university students, along with some professors passed buckets of lime, sand, and rocks up the mountain to fill up the letters. After six hours, only the Y had been completed, and the completion of the project was postponed and eventually abandoned, leaving only the Y out of the three letters. It's a very popular hike that's not too complicated in the sense of treacherous trail, but it does have some very steep inclines. So if you're not used to difficult hikes, be prepared for this one. The view at the top is gorgeous and provides an overview of the entire Utah Valley and the Utah Lake.

The Covey Center for the Arts

The Covey Center for the Arts is found in Center Street in Provo. Here you can come to enjoy different theatre performances. The remaining shows for this year are: Mark Twain's The Diaries of Adam and Even, The Alibis, and The Christmas Express. The Center also offers four different Art Exhibits you can visit. Lastly, it offers dance classes for all ages including Ballet, Jazz, Hip-Hop, and Contemporary dancing. If you're in the area be sure to check what are the current Art Exhibits and the days and times for the theatre shows.
